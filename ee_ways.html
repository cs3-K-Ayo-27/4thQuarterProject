Environmental Engineering

Air Quality


Environmental Engineering associates itself with air quality. Environmental Engineers design and implement pollution technologies. These include filters to remove harmful substances from daily industrial and transportation emissions before they are released into the atmosphere.

Water Treatment


Environmental Engineering also focuses on water treatment. Due to the rising pollution in water, environmental engineers design, develop, and implement various technologies and strategies to remove harmful contaminants from water sources. They play a vital role in maintaining our water safe.

Waste Management


Knowing the three famous R's, reduce, reuse, and recycle, Environmental Engineering also applies those. Environmental engineers work on waste reduction strategies, making sure that the resources they used are conserved. They work on recyclability to ensure that no product or resource is going to waste.

Air Quality


"An air quality engineer has numerous responsibilities, which generally fall into the following broad categories: Modeling and understanding pollution and its sources. Monitoring emissions and compliance with applicable regulations. Designing and implementing quality improvement solutions."

(Source: How Air Quality Engineers Help with Air Pollution Control)

Water Treatment


"Environmental engineers specializing in water also design wastewater treatment facilities to treat water after human use so that it can be returned to the environment without negatively impacting the ecology and downstream users."

(Source: Water Resources and Treatment | Environmental Engineering Program | University of Colorado Boulder)

Waste Management


Studies involving the extraction and reuse of useful substances from discarded items, garbage, or waste. The process of managing, and disposing of, wastes and hazardous substances through methodologies such as landfills, sewage treatment, composting, waste reduction, etc.

(Source: Environmental Engineering - Society for Science)
